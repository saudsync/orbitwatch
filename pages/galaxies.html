These iconic galaxies have shaped our understanding of the cosmos — from the structure of the universe to the existence of black holes, dark matter, and the speed of cosmic expansion.

Galaxy Groups & Clusters

Galaxies rarely travel alone — they group together under gravity into structures of increasing scale.

🏘️
Local Group
Galaxy Group

Our home galaxy group containing ~80 galaxies including the Milky Way, Andromeda, and Triangulum, spanning about 10 million light-years.

Members: ~80 galaxies · Span: 10M ly
🏙️
Virgo Cluster
Galaxy Cluster

The nearest large galaxy cluster to Earth, containing over 1,300 galaxies including the giant elliptical M87 with its famous black hole image.

Members: 1,300+ · Distance: 53.8M ly
🌆
Coma Cluster
Galaxy Cluster

One of the densest known galaxy clusters, famous for being one of the first places where dark matter was inferred from the unexpectedly fast motion of galaxies.

Members: ~1,000 · Distance: 321M ly
🌇
Perseus Cluster
Galaxy Cluster

One of the most massive objects in the known universe and the brightest X-ray cluster in the sky. Contains thousands of galaxies embedded in a vast cloud of hot plasma.

Members: ~190 bright · Distance: 240M ly
🌃
Laniakea Supercluster
Supercluster

Our home supercluster — identified in 2014. Contains the Milky Way along with ~100,000 other galaxies spread across 520 million light-years.

Galaxies: ~100,000 · Span: 520M ly
🌌
Bullet Cluster
Merging Cluster Pair

Two colliding galaxy clusters that provided the strongest direct evidence for the existence of dark matter — hot gas slows down but dark matter passes straight through.

Distance: 3.8B ly · Evidence: Dark Matter
